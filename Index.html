Pathways to Citizenship

MILITARY SERVICE &
U.S. CITIZENSHIP

Naturalization Ceremony

Military service is one of the most honored pathways to U.S. citizenship. Non-citizens who serve honorably may be eligible for expedited naturalization under Section 329 of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA).

Naturalization During Peacetime (INA § 328)

Legal permanent residents who have served at least one year on active duty may apply for naturalization with reduced physical presence requirements.

Expedited Naturalization During Hostilities (INA § 329)

Non-citizens who serve honorably during a designated period of hostilities may apply for citizenship immediately upon enlistment.

Green Card (LPR) Through Service

Certain military visa programs have allowed enlistment in exchange for lawful permanent residency. Speak with a recruiter for current program status.

Important: All immigration and naturalization benefits through the military are provided by the U.S. government at no cost to the service member. For official information visit uscis.gov.
